In this tutorial, you will learn how to create a basic gif in Procreate and then export it to be used as an animated sticker on Instagram.

Step 1: Plan out the frames


For this tutorial I will be demonstrating with a blooming flower.

The first step is to plan out the frames. Draw some guidelines in the directions you want the animation to follow, in this example I have a semi circle that I want the petals to follow as they open up. Use quick and basic shapes to plot out how much movement will be between each frame, and keep revising until the animation is smooth. It is always helpful if you can find a reference video with similar movement to what you are trying to achieve.

Step 2: Finalize the frames with line art


Now that you have an idea of where the frames will be, finalize them with solid lines. Create your intended shapes during this step, building off of the quick and basic shapes. Remember that onion skins exist, and can help with redrawing the frames.

Step 3: Add flat colours


Now for the fun part, choose your colour palette! Colour in each frame with a flat fill.

Step 4: Add shading and highlights


Finish off your colouring by picking some additional colours to add depth. For this example I have used a basic two colour gradient. Onion skins can help you again here to ensure details flow through the frames smoothly.

Step 5: Add extra details


For the final drawing step, add in any extra details you want. In this example I have added a stem to the flower. This is also a good time to colour the line art you made in step two, so that it does not stand out as much, or even remove it altogether.

Step 6: Exporting


If you are using Procreate you can export the animation as a Web Ready gif, but no matter what program you use, export the animation as a gif and send it to your mobile devices photo album.

Step 7: Create an Instagram story


Swipe to the right on Instagrams home page in their mobile app to create a post, and select the picture you want to upload to your story with the animation.

Step 8: Copy the animation


Go into your mobile devices photo album, and copy the animation. On an iPhone, you tap the share button at the bottom left of the animation to bring up an actions menu, and then press “copy photo”.

Step 9: Put it on your story


Now when you go back to Instagram, it already knows you have the animation in your clipboard so all you need to do is tap the popup in the bottom left and there you go! You have created a sticker for your Instagram story!
